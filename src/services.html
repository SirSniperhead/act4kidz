Help Services

Act for Kids proudly offers a range of support services to both children and their families!

Act for kids is an organisation dedicated to making kids lives better, and to do this we offer many different service ranging from therapy from abuse and neglect trauma to providing safe homes for those under child protection orders.

Integrated Therapy Services

Therapeutic Service for children,young people, families and/or carers

The Integrated Therapy Service (ITS) is a therapeutic service for children, young people, their families and/or carers who have behavioural, emotional and/or developmental difficulties due to trauma from abuse and neglect or who are at risk of harm. A full integrated team comprises of speech and language therapists, occupational therapists and psychologists.

Speech Therapy

Speech Pathologists help with communication, language and speech skills. This includes how children and young people understand and use language, and the impact that any difficulties may have upon relationships, learning and emotional adjustment.

Occupational Therapy

The aim of Occupational therapy is to enable children and young people to participate in the activities of everyday life that bring meaning and purpose to them. These include movement skills, play skills, and many of the skills needed for school readiness. Occupational therapists work with children, young people, their families and their communities to enhance their ability to engage in the activities that they want to, need to, or are expected to do, or by modifying the activities or the environment to allow for better engagement and participation.

Psychological services

Psychologists assess and support children and families with concerns about general development and learning, social and emotional skills, behavioural difficulties and relationships. Psychologists can work with children, young people, their families, and other important adults in their lives (e.g., schools), to manage and respond to traumatic experiences.

Intensive Family Support

Provides Case Management Support

Intensive Family Support (IFS) programs provide case management support to families at risk of entering the statutory child protection system. The IFS services accepts eligible referrals from the Family and Child Connect, Department of Child Safety, prescribed entities, community agencies and self-referrals from families.

IFS services can take referrals without consent from the Department of Child Safety and other prescribed entities. Referrals into this service must demonstrate the referred family has multiple and complex needs, has a child/ren under 18, are at risk of entering the child protection system if support is not obtained and would benefit from a case management service.

Depending on the service size, our intensive support services can also include allied health practitioners, in– home support workers, cultural advisors and specialist domestic and family violence practitioners.

What Does Family Support Do?

  • Addressing issues such as domestic violence, mental health and substance abuse.
  • Developing stronger life skills such as setting routines, budgeting and managing the household.
  • Parenting support and strategies to build positive family relationships
  • Managing stress to cope with daily pressures.
  • Providing child health and wellbeing resources.
  • Linking into services that support your child’s development.
  • Connecting you with local services, playgroups and other social networks
  • Supporting families with their cultural needs.
  • Anything else that worries you

Early Education Program (EEP)

Educational intervention service for kids struggling to start school due to abuse or neglect

The Early Education Program (EEP) is a unique pre-school educational intervention service for children who struggle to attain skills necessary to start school as a result of abuse or neglect.

The Townsville, Brisbane and Cairns program was developed through a partnership between Act for Kids and Education Queensland. The teachers are a part of the Townsville, Brisbane and Cairns teams and work closely with practitioners in these centres to integrate interventions specific to individual children into the curriculum.

The EEP is open four days a week and children aged 2–5 years attend full or half days at the pre-school learning the skills they need for primary school.

How Can the Early Education Program help?

A registered teacher develops individual learning plans for each child to ensure they reach their full potential. Children also receive support from a multi-disciplinary team of practitioners that can include psychologists, speech and language pathologists, occupational therapists, nurses, social workers and like disciplines. The program works with the parents, caregivers and other family members to help ensure the child is safe and happy and that they experience positive early development and interventions.

SafeKIDS

Provides safe and culturally appropriate home environments for kids

Many Aboriginal children and young people under child protection orders or investigation are placed in care outside of their communities making maintaining family, community and cultural connections impossible.

Locations

In partnership with The Aboriginal Corporation of West Coast Cape York Peninsula Traditional Land Owners (WCTO) and the Department of Communities, Child Safety and Disability Services, Act for Kids operates safe houses in Aurukun, Kowanyama, Napranum and Pormpuraaw.

Act for Kids operates a safe house in Doomadgee. It is expected we will transition control and management of the program to the traditional owners. Due to the success of the safe houses in the Cape and Gulf, Act for Kids was also asked to set up an additional safe house in Cairns.
